About Uwem Useh

Sir Uwem Jonah Useh is an indigene of Ikot Umo Essien Village, Odoro Ikot Clan in Essien Udim L.G.A of Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria.
e is an Assistant Director and immediate past Head of Climate Change Mitigation, Federal Ministry of Environment, Nigeria but currently seconded as the Senior Technical Assistant (Policy & Strategic Planning) to the Federal Capital Territory Administration under Economic Planning, Revenue Generation, and Public Private Partnership (EPRG-PPPS).
Sir Uwem is an environmental professional who holds an M.Sc. in Environmental Resources Management with a Ph.D. in view. His over 20 years of work experience in science, energy, environment, and sustainable development spans government, private sector, and NGO. As a Federal Civil Servant, he won an award as the best creative and innovative writer in the Federal Civil Service of Nigeria during the 2016 Nigeria Civil Service Week. Beyond his core academic trainings, he has earned many professional affiliations and some of his job achievements include – Serving as the Nigeria’s National Focal Point on West Africa Coastal Area Management Program (WACA), Coordinating the development and implementation of Multi-sectoral Resilience Plan for the development of Coastal Communities in Nigeria, Support for Energy-Efficient Air Conditioners in Nigeria, Development of the Comprehensive Plan of Action for the Management of Flood Disaster in Nigeria, Nigeria's National Projects, Policy & Action Plans and Strategies of the Paris Agreement including Nigeria’s Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC), and Energy Transition Plan etc.
As the Director of Music at the Methodist Church Nigeria, Diocese of Kubwa, Abuja, Nigeria, Sir Uwem has composed many gospel anthems and hymns. Because of his contributions to the development of music in the Christendom, he was given the highest award as the Knight of Charles Wesley, KCW by the Conference of Methodist Church Nigeria in 2018. He was a member of the LOC, All African Conference of Churches that was hosted by Nigeria in November 2023 and he was the Chairman of the Music Sub-Committee for the Conference. Sir Uwem Useh is happily married to Lady Mercy, Ph.D and the marriage is blessed with three children (a girl and two boys). Academically, he has six publications to his credit as well as publication of five (5) textbooks including a hymnal with histories of popular Christian hymns. Presently, Sir Uwem is conducting a research study to investigate the multifaceted impacts of mangrove forest depletion on climate change in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria. This study aims to assess the influence of the mangrove cover changes on CO2 emissions as well as land surface temperature (LST) under different anthropogenic activities, in order to better comprehend the role of these endangered ecosystems for GHG emissions, temperature regulation and carbon sequestration.
